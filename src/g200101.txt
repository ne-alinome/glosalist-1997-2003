A little origami.....
This paper is folded. It is unfolded. (....... positive number of folds 
..........   zero folds)
fold this paper! Leave it folded! Unfold it! .... positive folding actions 
(........... zero folding actions ........ negative folding actions)
"Un" = zero or negative/opposite   
ne akti*refrain      .......... zero act
ne foto*dull (not clear/bright) ......... little light
no-face*demolish .......... negative make
no-facili*difficult  ........ negative make
no-kine*motionless .......... zero action
no-kine*not moving
no-kine*stationary
zero*nought
nuli*nil
nuli*no
nuli*nothing
nuli*repeal
We have "ne", "no", "nuli" "zero" for much the same meaning.
Both "un" in Eng and "ne/no" in Glosa are rather ambiguous in these examples. 
It has been suggested ( by Ron Clark, I think) that "no" should be used for 
zero meaning and "ne" for negative. I agree but as there is the confusion in 
the core dictionary, I prefer to use "ne(g)" and "ze(r)" in Sti Glosa as they 
are clearer. For the moment I shall stick to these while I experiment.
To encourage new-comers the dictionary should be kept as short and clear as 
possible.

> We have "ne", "no", "nuli" "zero" for much the same meaning.
> Both "un" in Eng and "ne/no" in Glosa are rather ambiguous

It is similar in all the eurolangs and maybe all languages;
common particles attach themselves to verbs or nouns,
without logical rigor. We must live with it. In math we have
two simple inverse operations, one additive and one multiplicative;
they can be seen as resembling negation (non-) and opposition (un-).

Wouldn't it be nice to have two such corresponding prefix particles
in an auxlang? We need them both as adverbs and determiners.
Word order in Glosa is fixed, lessening possible adverb confusion.
But to do this systematically exposes a further problem:

There are many (perhaps 100) common particles that form subsets
of similar syntactic usage, e.g. "none one some all",
"not very more most less least", "with without", "and or else but".
Translating to Greco-Latin, there are problems like "sin = without"
versus "syn = with", or "in = within" versus "in- = not".

Since they are so frequent in usage, they must be easily
distinguishable phonetically, and work well as prefixes.
I think it all adds up to a choice of whether to perpetuate
the eurolang confusion, or not. Loglan/Lojban is the only
auxlang to choose the latter option.

For recognizability, "ne" and "no" are clear winners for negation.
But for opposition there is only confusion: de- des- dis- a- ab- ap-
an- in- un- anti- contra- etc. (I am considering adverbial forms
because they are most productive. Adjectives like "none, null"
are combinations of adverb+adjective, "not one", etc.)

If I were designing a conlang (again), I'd want to use bisyllabic
roots like "nega, anti, mono" and free compounding for everything.
With primary stress it sounds good, with hyphens it's easy to read.
But then I want to have all the "de- con- re-" prefixes too,
and some suffixes like -t and -nd and -s ... so it's non-possible.

   Alexander, tu posi habe dificili ko u mode de krea plu Glosa verbi.

   Ki retro, ad u prima loka de plu Glosa verb, na nece refere a plu paleo Latino e Greko.  Pluso, kron Ron Clark pa krea u lexikon de Glosa, an pa uti plu idea de plu origi krea-pe de plu-ci lingua.

   An pa dice a mi ke plu Greko-pe pa habe un eklekti lingua; in qi singo verbi pote habe plu pusi verbi intra id.  Plu-ci pusi verbi, posi de bi, alo tri, litera, dona u mero de u toto koncepa de u ma mega kompleti verbi. U-ci abili, te tekto plu komplexi verbi ex poli pusi mero, pa do plu Greko-pe u flex-abili ko pli idea, e plu verbi; in qi te expresi mu. 

  Alterno, plu Latino-dice-pe pa uti mu plu verbi iso ma solidi ra, e ne developo plu filosofi-pe, ke na ski ex Greko kultura.  Intra u Latino lingua na vide u kombina de plu existe verbi te face plu komplexi verbi, pluso un uti de plu pre-fixa e post-fixa.

  E so, kron plu Glosa autori-pe pa kre mu neo lingu, mu pa uti holo de plu-ci metodi te forma plu ge-nece moderni verbi.


  U ~dis~ intra ~disolve~ e ~diskursi~ eqa `de -` alo `in id plu mero` alo `ex u -`.  Ko ~disolve~ na vide u solve de u solidi objekti in id plu mero, usuali intra hidro.

  Ko ~diskursi~ na habe u meta-fora uti de u verbi ~kursi~: vice puta de u persona; qi kursi peri u loka, na habe u pikto de u menta; qi kursi peri holo de u regio de u subjekti.  Posto kine dia u subjekti, u persona pote grafo de an menta viagio, dia u-ci subjekti: an publiko an plu idea iso u ~diskursi~ (ex u - kursi).  Na pote uti u homo verbi te deskribe u ge-dice `kursi` de mo, alo ma, persona dia u subjekti. [discourse; discussion]

  #tritus#, ex u Latino verbi-bibli, habe u semani de uno-ra; qi gene lase ex u surfacia po frika.  ~detritus~ eqa qod gene ge-lase po un objekti pa gene ge-frika (de - trite) [from the .. wearing].  

  Intra _Glosa 6000_ na detekti      TRITE   old, worn.


  Te detekti plu-ci semani de plu Glosa verbi, mi pa merka pusi verbi-bibli de Latino-lingua e Greko-lingua.  Mi pa duce plu pedi-kadu de Ron Clark, te logi plu raisona pro Glosa verbi selekti.
