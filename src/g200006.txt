>Karo plu Glosa-pe, 
>
*  Karo Glosa-pe,
  [dear Glosa-person]
   This form will do for both singular and plural, where the usage is
obvious.  It is 'softer' and less pompous to avoid excessive
grammaticisation, where this can be elided.

>Mi grafo brevi de progresi de mi Deutsch Glosa reti-pagina 
>   www.tu-harburg.de/~smms1406/glosa.htm: 
>
*  Mi brevi grafo de u progresi de mi Deutsch Glosa reti-pagina
[I briefly write about the progress of my German Glosa web-page]

   ~brevi~ as a simple modifier goes before the 'verb' it modifies.
   The ~u~ before ~progresi~ !forces! the recognition that we are reading
it as a 'noun' (perhaps it is not necessary, but prevents any possible
question in the mind of the reader).

>* U pagina nu habe bi parti.  U proto dice ko emotio e tenta motiva u 
*           U proto mero dice ko emotio; e tenta motiva u lekto-pe
           [the first piece speaks with emotion, and  ....... ]

   To avoid possible reading of ~proto dice~ as "first speaking", it is
better to include the !missing! 'noun': while using ~parti~ might offend
through repetition, the use of ~mero~ [part, piece] provides both the
'noun', which ~proto~ modifies, and the variety required by style.
   I'd say that the use of ~;~ is worthwhile to indicate the start of a new
clause.  Some find the ~;~ ugly, and prefer the ~,~ which is a common
clause delimiter in English.  The Glosa authors decreed _;_ for the start
of a new clause, and _,_ where necessary to indicate the start of a new
phrase. 
   Because of Glosa's syntax-based grammar, in which there are no
Part-of-Speech markers, the punctuation assumes grammatical significance ..
providing automatic disambiguation of written script.  In the spoken form,
alternatively, pause and vocal inflection indicate sentence structure to
the listener.

>    Po-co textu habe no komika, sed ma faktu-info.  
*    Po-co, u textu habe objecti, e habe ma faktu info.
 [after that, the text is objective, and has more factual information]
  
   The _,_ ensures that we do not read the ~po-co~ as modifying the
~textu~.  The ~u~ ensures that we know the word before the 'verb' ~habe~
must be a 'noun'. 
   ~habe objecti~ could be translated as "has objectiveness", but obviously
we use the more usual "is objective".
   I feel that ~e~ is more logical than ~sed~ in this construction.
   ~faktu info~ is a modifier/'noun' pair and calls for no hyphen.  The
hyphen, which is used to form compounds, usually joins words of equivalent
function, eg NOUN-NOUN: ~soni-info~ [information as sound]
   ~ma faktu info~ is a standard NOUN phrase (comparative-modifier-NOUN).

>* Major-pagina ne du habe info de hetero plu auxi-lingua.  
*  U major-pagina ne habe info de plu hetero auxi-lingua.
[the main page (does) not have information about the other auxiliary
languages]
   
   In this case, it just flows better for me with the less abrupt ~U
major-pagina~.  It is not _wrong_ without the ~U~.
   Using the continuous tense does not apply here: ~ne du habe~ [= is not
having].
   ~de~ starts a Prepositional Phrase, which is structurally a Noun Phrase.
 In Noun Phrases there is a fairly standard sequence for the elements:-
    Determinant - Numerator - Quantifier - Modifier(s) -'noun'
 eg    plu          tri         hetero       Latino     auxi-lingua

> U lekto-pe debi koncentra pro Glosa. 
*  U lekto-pe debi koncentra * de Glosa.
  [the reader ought (to) concentrate * <about> Glosa]

   This is an easy construction in English, and we could use ~epi~ which
would be the translation of an English usage: ~de~ is the 'general- 
purpose' Glosa preposition, and this is probably the right word to use.
   The _*_ marks a missing, hidden NOUN.  We are not concentrating Glosa,
nor are we just concentrating about Glosa, we are, in fact, concentrating
_something_ on Glosa.  
   U lekto-pe debi koncentra an menta a Glosa.
  [the reader should concentrate his mind towards Glosa]
  ~pro~ would possibly be equally as good here.

> U-ci info pa gene loki in hetero datu-fa (gfaq.htm).  
* Un info de plu hetero auxi-lingua, pa gene loka intra u hetero datu-fa

   I found that the earlier statement was so far from ~U-ci~ that the link
was not still there.
   ~intra u hetero~ seemed necessary here = "inside an-other"
