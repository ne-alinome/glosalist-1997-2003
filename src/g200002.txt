*   The list is almost endless; it more, or less, began in the Renaissance, with the members, of the newly set up Royal Society, deciding there ought to be a language for ALL scientists.

    Now it seems like a bazaar, from which one selects the language one likes.

    However, there is a catch: nobody can decide which is the best of the Planned Languages; but, if the world is to have _A_ Second Language, only one of them can be successful in achieving this quest.


>   How does Glosa compare to Esperanto? 

*   For your purposes, travelling widely, and meeting new people - with whom you can communicate directly - Esperanto WINS.

    If, on the other hand, you take a longer, possibly more intuitive, view, you could see that the race is not yet won, and that Glosa could be a more suitable modern language.

    Esperanto has the infrastructure, a vast library of books, and an army of speakers.  The only drawback is that it is a bit hard to learn. With 99% of the world's population ignorant of the International Auxiliary Language (IAL) hypothesis, either way, Interlanguage is still not even a minor area of human endeavour.


>What are the advantages of Glosa over Esperanto?

*   Mainly intellectual, and possibly psychological.  Glosa, designed a century after Esperanto, embodies many of the new views on Linguistics.

So, Glosa is like English, which, of the Euro languages, has the least morphological grammar .. but it progressed with this trend to the Chinese position, where there is virtually no grammatical change to the words. 

    If your psychology sets you up to follow cleary defined patterns and rules, then you will be in tune with the way Esperanto works; however, if you vere towards the creative use of language, you are likely to appreciate the "open plan" nature of Glosa.

    The Esperanto dictionary has been through a number of revisions and is fairly well settled; the Glosa dictionary (Glosa 6000), is reasonably well set, but contains a few inconsistencies, and much more importantly, there are vast areas of human endeavour for which Glosa lexicons have not been derived.  The Glosa advantage is that, in coming from the Classical roots - from which the science terminology also comes - the derivation of new terms is fairly straight-forward.

replaces CENTI for "hundred"; CENTI becomes "hundredth". This makes some

   CENTI = hundredth
   CENTIMETRA = centimeter, centimetre

   HEKTO = hundred
   HEKTOPEDI = centipede

   MILI = thousandth
   MILIMETRA = millimeter, millimetre

   KARAKTERI = trait
   UNITA = unit

   NIMA = name
   NIMA-BILETA = label

   SUMA-ME = calculator

   GE-LIMITA = exclusive

   PRANDIA = supper
