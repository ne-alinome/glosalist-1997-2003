before V and joined by a hyphen. we could leave out the hyphen, we could use

the beginning of the sentence. I should like to know as soon as possible that

an explanation of the format:-
"+" introduces a glosa word, "*" intro english and "^" intros a comment
"VC" shows a g word that begins with a vowel, glosa tries to have CVCVCV>>>>>
CV as this is easier to pronounce generally and like this, the CV alternation 
is preserved between words. 
"PC" indicates (usually) that a plosive (b c d g k p t ) is followed by a 
consonant. some speakers find this difficult at times.
q, x, r, l are included in the glosa.
some people, including me to some extent, prefer not to have a couple of 
single signs representing pairs of sounds and think it would be more logical 
to have "kw", and "ks"; the latter is a PC and might be better avoided.
for some speakers in east asia, l and r are not so easy to distinguish so 
that if there are two g words that only differ with "l" and "r", those 
consonants perhaps should be avoided. the number in square brackets [] show 
how often the word has been used so far in PGN or glosalist (not an 
exhaustive count) and could be used as a guide in choosing.
sometimes, a concept is represented by more than one word, the comments may 
help choice.  
    
I shall update the list about every fortnight or month. if you write in g, 
you will influence the list.....
sid

pa skope pagina tri e ma e ma pagina. mi pa pote logi plu de pagina e kusdev

farina es mo taxo de pulve, ge-uti de face pani.

> lice

sti pote es dona pote. lase es ne-sto. lice es u lega lase.

extende es ma-mega longi, alo ma-mega area.

mi pa apo ab 'sekreti' de mi lista, kausa id es homo

Mi ne ski. Posi na ne nece 'figura'.

dia es intra. trans es epi. mi dromo dia u porta. mi dromo

* Hello Tom,

    It's been quite a while.

    Well, I still prefer Glosa over Esperanto, but if it was a question
of adopting a fully-set-up IAL to-morrow, I'd probably have to go along
with Esperanto.

    Hopes and expectations:-

       . I am somewhat back-pedalling on my involvement, but do answer
letters on the Glosalist, around 50% in Glosa. 

       . I feel that most Glosa-pe are "dictionarists" not
'communicators': this augers badly for the growth of Glosa.  

       . My guess is that most people, used to the shoe-horn effects of
inflections and idioms in the national languages and most Planned
Languages, find Glosa a bit raw, with the need to think in 'pure terms'
before speaking.

       . So, it is this 'concept language' element of Glosa that has
prevented its ^taking off^ among scholars and intellectuals.

       . Nowadays, when I write in Glosa, what comes out is a slightly
stilted version of translated English; however, much cognition goes into
the process - mostly unconscious now - selecting from my Glosa vocab, and
jostling the words into a suitable Glosa syntax .. which, as a result of
the operation of some "universals," comes out looking like an almost
word-for-word translation of a version of English.

       . There are about ten websites featuring Glosa, nowadays, and once
there was only mine: the newer ones are in German, Russian, French and
some in English.  So things appear to have gone beyond the critical mass
needed to make the 'Glosa movement' self-perpetuating.

       . There has been renewed discussion to standardise the "Basic
Glosa Dictionary" (~1500 words), and subsequently to release further
standards at ~2500 and ~6500 words.  This is essential if Glosa is to be
regularised, and to avoid the  inconsistencies found in the original
publications.  Ron and Wendy attempted to carry on with the existing,
inconsistent word-lists, because of the cost of changing the paper-print
runs.  However this matter, of retooling costs, does not come up with
electronic formats.

       . Wendy has recovered from losing Ron, and has started to function
again, but is financially limited.  So, although we do not want to form
schisms, the Net groupings are moving off in there own directions; Wendy
is, however, still nominal head of the Glosa moity.

       . Were I twenty years younger, and more vigorous, perhaps I would
try a lot harder: I do try to be true to Ron's original visions for the
linguistic form, and for the ethic of low-cost auxilliary language for
all; however, we cannot avoid being in the hands of the more affluent,
who come across as also beimg more 'academic' in their approach.

       . The Uganda experiment is failing somewhat because the Africans
are not accessible to the Glosa-pe on the Internet.  Hand-held aerograms
seem to have gone out of fashion; connected Glosa-pe do not see support
of non-connected Glosa communities as part of their brief.  I have had
one computer printer 'lost in the mail' while trying to get an A$80.00
Toshiba luggable over to Banobi Herbert, who runs a Mountain Gorilla
sanctury as a Tourist attraction and wealth generator in Uganda.


       . However, the sum-up says that, with war-drums beating, this is
NOT the time for world-building; and, the sort of 'globally
communicating' world, that Glosa heralds, is not in line with the Power
Brokers' idea of "Globalisation."  In short, marching out of step for too
long hurts the legs.  It is an open question whether altruists have any
hope of realising their vision of a better world - in the face of the
extreme centralisation of wealth, industrial might, and political 
power.


But we keep trying,


Robin Gaskell

I think the 'concept' part is acceptable and even fashionable.
Analytic style may be the easiest for the most people to learn;
it's used quite a bit in Chinese and even English. Its basic
grammar design is certainly workable, though some of the details
(e.g. the semicolons to mark clauses) are unnecessarily bizarre.

Maybe the problem (if there is one) is the quality of the vocabulary.
If just the internationally known (European) words were allowed,
with clear rules as to what form they should take (as in LsF),
it might actually become popular on the internet -- which is
all that matters anymore.

I scribbled:

>> Maybe the problem (if there is one) is the quality of the vocabulary.
>> If just the internationally known (European) words were allowed,
>> with clear rules as to what form they should take (as in LsF),
>> it might actually become popular on the internet -- which is
>> all that matters anymore.

Alex replied:

> But present vocabulary of Glosa "gives meaning to any scientific term". It's
> very useful, is not it?

I think that, since the time of Hogben's original Interglossa idea,
Greek scientific roots have become less internationally recognizable
and more idiosyncratic (non-systematic, opaque, iconic) in actual use.
I've examined many lists of Greek "combining forms" like BIO-, HYDRO-,
-GRAPHY, -TOMY, etc, and there just aren't enough of them to make an
operational language. Glosa needed to add Latin roots to complete its
vocabulary, and they are very recognizable to the largest percentage
of interested people, whereas Greek is not (especially after phonetic
adjustments), and usually conflicts with better-known Latin roots.

So, why not eliminate the troublesome Greek and instead define a few
easy principles to allow anyone to derive the vocabulary as needed?
This was done in Peano's Interlingua, again in Occidental/Interlingue,
and yet again in Gode's Interlingua. For example, from my pocket Italian
dictionary it's obvious that English FAVOR = Italian FAVORE, nicely
Glosa-like and international; but checking the official G vocab:

FAVO       favor
GEFAVO     favorite
FAVORI     favorable
FAVORITA   favorite

One can easily see the difficulty here, and a possible solution.

U frasi mo in PGN 86 es "U developo de energi-ma celu teknologi nu-du tako 
avanti in Islanda."
Na nu-du habe u sti de akti-verba-grupo, na nu-du nece detekti u kefa-verba. 
U-ci, id es u verba ante plu verba "in Islanda". Id es "avanti". "Tako" nu-du 
auxi u semani de "avanti" e "in Island" pluso nu-du auxi "avanti". Il no es u 
"objekti" verba-grupo, u grupo ante "nu-du es u "subjekti" grupo e u verba 
ante plu verba "de energi ....." es u subjekti kefa verba. Pan u-co, mi nu-du 
facile de logi.
Na nu-du habe:- 
intro de subjekti - (no auxi verba) - kefa verba - "de" plus auxi grupo - 
intro de akti-grupo - auxi - kefa - "in" plus auxi - no objekti
