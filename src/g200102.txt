At 13:59 28/01/2001 EST, Syd Pidd grafo:
>mi detekti qui "mo" = "one" et "furniture" et "ma" = "more" and "material". 
>U-ci es anti u buta de Glosa, mi puta qi "mo" =1 e "mobile" alo "mobi" debi 
>=furniture, ma=more sed materia alo mate=material..................
*   Il es u difere inter ~mo~ e ~-mo~
    U toto separa verbi ~mo~ habe u semani de "1"
    Kron na, alo u verbi-procesa puta-me, detekti u ~-mo~ ge-fixa ad u
hetero verbi ko u jugo-signi, na, alo u puta-me, pa gene ge-informa de id
semani; qi eqa ~mobili~.  U puta-me pa habe id instruo, e u persona gene
sko de id, e nuli-tem no-memo id. 

    U voka dice ~Ma ligni-ma!~ sembla indika ke uno-pe vo habe plura mero
de u ligni; mu du uti.

>sugesti pluso :- 
>+volu*impulse          pulsa [push]; te akti per volu
>+volu*need             vo habe; nece
>+volu*require          nece habe 
>+volu*voluntary        an volu ergo; an es u volu-ergo-pe
>+volu*want             vo habe; nece habe  (qod es u dina de tu desira?)
>+volu*will             id es mi volu te akti id; te habe dina volu
>+volu*willing          an es u volu participa-pe
>+volu*willingness      place monstra tu volu-ra te akti u-ci
>+volu*wish             mi volu es pro paca
>"impulse,need,require...............wish" pan  gene kolekte in English ad 
>"require" ante translate a glosa. 9 coredictionary verba nu equ 1.
>  
*   Il es u problema, sed nuli u serioso mo.  Te striktu u verbi lista, Ron
Clark pa *seka plu gona*.  Ko u-ci kaso, an pa decide ke plu koncepa de
"wish" e "will" es ta proxi mu pote habe mo Glosa verbi.  
    Po plu nece, de uno-pe, sio kreske ma de u basi 6000 verbi, an fu
detekti plu hetero verbi te lice an monstra u difere inter "willing" e
"wishing".

    U dificili; qi Ron Clark pa kausa ko u 'verbi translati' diktionari, es
un idea ke singu verbi intra mo lingua fu nece habe un eqivalu verbi intra
u hetero lingua.  Pro multi basi, Ron pa produce u simpli verbi a verbi
diktionari, sed te korekti uti u lingua, mi logi ke na nece skope plu
semani de plu koncepa intra plu verbi, ne simpli uti singu verbi iso u nima
bileta.
    Te cerka u ~volu~ mi sugere ke tu klude tu oku, e fixa tu menta
koncerne qod tu fu akti.  Kron tu detekti qod tu nece akti, tu pa detekti
tu ~volu.~
    U tristi faktu es ke plura-pe habe forti resolve, e so habe dina
~volu~; tem hetero-pe tenta akti no-reali ra, alo habe pusi resolve, e mu
~volu~ habe no-dina.  U-ci difere posi don explika pro "will" e "wish".  Ka
u limita; ge-uti kron forma u Glosa verbi lista, tem na komence uti Glosa,
na nece uti deskribe in na uti de pu verbi. EG,
  An vo asura akti id, sed an amika pa habe nuli volu te akti u projekti.
[he willed certainly to do it, but his friend had no wish to do the project]
 He would certainly do it, but his friend had no wish to do the project.

Saluta,

Robin Gaskell

Here is a list of Greek and Latin roots (column 1)
that have cognates in English (column 2),
reduced to a bi-syllabic CVCV pattern (column 3).

The reductions are straightforward, except the final vowel.
This is -u or -i for Latin, -o or -e for Greek; -a could
be used for Latin feminines or reserved for Germanic/other.

There is nearly a 4-to-1 ratio of Latin to Greek roots.
This simple heuristic enables unambiguous mixing of
both word stocks, avoiding problems such as the
Greek "syn = with" vs. Latin "sin = without".

scurra    buffoon   scuri
scutum    shield    scutu
sebum     fat       sebu
sectus    cut       sectu
saeculum  secular   saecu
sedere    sit       sedi
sedulus   diligent  sedu
selene    moon      sele
semen     seed      semni
senex     old       seni
sensus    feel      sensu

titillare tickle    titi
titulus   title     titu
tolerare  tolerate  toli
temnein   cut       tome
tumbos    tomb      tumbo
tonos     tone      tono
tonsus    clipped   tonsu
tonsa     oar       tonsi
topos     place     topo
tornus    turn      tornu
torpere   numb      torpi
torrere   hot       tori

I think approximately 2000 such roots would and should constitute
the true core vocabulary of any IAL designed to be easiest for
European and American people to learn and use. I'm undecided
whether adding Germanic roots would help more than hurt.
